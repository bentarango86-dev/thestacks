# The Stacks — User Guide

A personal vinyl record collection tracker. Add your records, browse them as genre "stacks," and access your collection from any device.

## Getting started

1. Open the app link you were given.
2. On first visit, you'll see a sign-in screen. Tap **"Need an account? Sign up"**, enter an email and password, and submit.
3. Check your email for a confirmation link. Click it, then come back and sign in.
4. Your collection is empty at first — start adding records.

### Installing it as an app on your phone

**iPhone:** Open the link in **Safari** (must be Safari, not Chrome). Tap the Share icon → **Add to Home Screen**. It'll appear as its own icon and open full-screen.

**Android:** Open the link in Chrome. You'll either see an automatic "Install app" banner, or you can tap the menu (⋮) → **Install app**.

Once installed, your data stays in sync with the web version — it's the same account either way.

## Adding a record

Tap **+ Add Record**. You have three ways to fill it in:

- **Search by name** — type the artist and album in the "Look up on MusicBrainz" box and hit Search. Pick the right result and most fields fill in automatically, including cover art if it's available.
- **Scan the barcode** — tap **📷 Scan**, allow camera access, and hold the record's barcode steady in frame. It'll look the release up automatically once it reads the code.
- **Type the barcode number** — if scanning doesn't work, you can type the UPC digits into the barcode field and hit Look up instead.
- **Enter everything manually** — just fill out the form yourself if a record doesn't turn up in either lookup.

Whichever way you start, everything is editable afterward — correct anything the lookup got wrong before saving.

## Browsing your collection

The app opens on **Stacks** — your records grouped into piles by genre, each showing a cover on top with a hint of other covers peeking out behind it. Tap a stack to open it up and see every record in that genre.

- **★ Set as stack cover** — inside an opened stack, each record card has a star button. Starring a record makes it the "face" of that genre's pile.
- **Search or filter** — typing in the search box, or picking a specific genre from the dropdown, jumps you straight into that filtered view. Use **← Back to Stacks** to return to the pile view.
- **Sort** — Recently added, Artist A–Z, Year, or Value, from the dropdown.

## Editing and deleting

Every record card has a pencil (✎) and an X. Pencil opens it for editing; X deletes it after a confirmation — deletion is permanent, there's no undo.

## Security

Tap **🔒 Security** in the header to turn on two-factor authentication (2FA). Scan the QR code with an authenticator app (Google Authenticator, Authy, 1Password, etc.), enter the 6-digit code it gives you to confirm, and from then on you'll need that code plus your password to sign in.

**Important:** there's no backup/recovery code for this. If you lose access to your authenticator app, you'll be locked out. Keep the QR code or setup key somewhere safe if you turn this on.

## Notes on your data

- Your collection is private to your account — nobody else who uses this app can see your records, and you can't see theirs.
- Lookups (search, barcode, cover art) need an internet connection. Browsing your existing collection works offline once the app has loaded.
- If something looks wrong after an update, try fully closing the app and reopening it — occasionally it takes a moment to catch up on that.
